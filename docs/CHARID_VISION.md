# CharID Vision

> **Superseded for product decisions by [CHARID_VISION_V3.md](./CHARID_VISION_V3.md)** — the governing Creator Operating System vision.  
> This document remains a **one-page appendix** for competitor contrast and onboarding.

Founder reference — use V3 before adding features, partnerships, or AI scope.

---

## 1. What is CharID?

CharID is a **home for creators and worldbuilders** to develop, organize, and publish original work.

The core loop:

**Character → World → Story → Continuity → Portfolio → Publishing**

Creators build **canon** (bibles, reference assets, slot roles), link characters across worlds and stories, and share finished work through a public portfolio when they choose. AI may assist reference and visualization later — it is a tool inside that loop, not the product itself.

CharID is a **character identity and creative dossier platform**, not a prompt box or media factory.

---

## 2. Who is it for?

- **Worldbuilders** building settings, rules, and lore over time
- **Character creators** who need identity, visuals, and consistency — not one-off images
- **Storytellers** connecting characters, worlds, and narrative in one place
- **Indie creators** who want ownership, privacy by default, and a portfolio they control
- **Future collaborators** who remix only when the original creator opts in

Not for: prompt tourists, bulk AI art farms, or users who only want a single generated image with no archive.

---

## 3. What makes it different from Midjourney?

| Midjourney | CharID |
|------------|--------|
| Prompt → image | Bible → assets → story → portfolio |
| Session-based generations | Persistent characters and worlds |
| Gallery of outputs | Canon with slot roles and continuity |
| Community feed as default | **Private by default**; publish when ready |

Midjourney optimizes for **beautiful images**. CharID optimizes for **coherent creative identity over months and years**.

---

## 4. What makes it different from Kling?

| Kling | CharID |
|-------|--------|
| Video generation | Long-form creative infrastructure |
| Clips and motion | Characters, worlds, stories, chapters |
| Consumable media | Owned IP and publishing |

Kling makes **moments**. CharID holds the **universe those moments belong to**. Video may come later as a credit-gated story tool — not the center of the product.

---

## 5. What makes it different from ChatGPT?

| ChatGPT | CharID |
|---------|--------|
| General conversation | Structured creative workspaces |
| Ephemeral threads | Durable bibles and asset libraries |
| No native portfolio | Public `/u/[username]` publishing |
| Broad knowledge | Deep context: character + world + story graph |

ChatGPT is a **thinking partner**. CharID is a **creative archive and identity system** with optional AI inside project context — not open-ended chat as the primary interface.

---

## 6. What should never change?

1. **Creators own their IP** — CharID does not own characters, worlds, stories, uploads, or project-generated assets ([CREATOR_ECONOMY_AND_RIGHTS.md](./CREATOR_ECONOMY_AND_RIGHTS.md))
2. **Private by default** — public, discoverable, and remixable are explicit opt-in
3. **Canon over chaos** — bibles and slot assignments are source of truth; AI suggests, creators decide
4. **No unlimited AI** — credits and tiers before any generation ships; no runaway API spend
5. **Not an AI image generator** — homepage and core UX lead with identity and worldbuilding, not prompts
6. **Safety and moderation** — uploads and AI outputs are scanned; founders can review flagged content
7. **The core loop** — Character → World → Story → Portfolio; features must strengthen this chain or they wait

---

## 7. What creator values guide product decisions?

| Value | In practice |
|-------|-------------|
| **Ownership** | Export, delete, commercial rights on paid tiers; no training on private work without consent |
| **Control** | Visibility, remix policy, and publish are creator choices |
| **Continuity** | One character stays consistent across worlds and stories |
| **Craft** | Bibles, completeness, asset roles — respect the work of building |
| **Trust** | Clear billing, credit costs, support, and honest AI labeling |
| **Home, not hustle** | Feel like a dossier and studio — not a feed, race, or slot machine |

**Decision test:** *Does this help a creator own and publish their world — or does it turn CharID into another generation site?* If the latter, defer or cut.

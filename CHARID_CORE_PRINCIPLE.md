# CharID Core Principle

## Mission

CharID exists to maintain creative consistency.

Not to replace creators.

Not to generate random content.

Not to be another AI chat app.

CharID is the memory system for creative projects.

---

## Core Consistency Layers

### Character Consistency

A character should remain consistent across:

- Images
- Stories
- Chapters
- Scenes
- Panels
- Pages
- Films
- Future media

CharID stores:

- Appearance
- Personality
- Voice
- Relationships
- Backstory
- Reference images

AI should always have access to this context.

---

### World Consistency

Worlds should maintain:

- Locations
- Rules
- History
- Technology
- Magic systems
- Organizations
- Culture

AI should understand the world before generating content.

---

### Story Consistency

Stories should maintain:

- Plot
- Character arcs
- Events
- Timeline
- Relationships

AI should not contradict previous story events.

---

### Scene Consistency

Scenes should maintain:

- Characters present
- Environment
- Clothing
- Mood
- Time of day
- Recent events

AI should generate scenes that fit existing context.

---

### Visual Consistency

Characters and environments should remain visually recognizable.

This includes:

- Character appearance
- Clothing
- Art style
- Environment design

CharID should become the visual memory layer for AI image generation.

---

---

## AI Philosophy

AI assists.

Creators create.

CharID remembers.

The purpose of AI inside CharID is not to replace creativity.

The purpose is to help maintain consistency and reduce repetitive work.

---

## Product Test

Every future feature should answer:

Does this improve consistency?

If yes, it belongs.

If no, reconsider building it.

---

## Long-Term Vision

CharID becomes the persistent creative memory layer between creators and AI.

A creator can return months or years later and continue building without losing continuity.

Characters remain consistent.

Worlds remain consistent.

Stories remain consistent.

AI remains informed.

That is the purpose of CharID.
